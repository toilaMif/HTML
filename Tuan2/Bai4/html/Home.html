Pressing Your Memories

Scrapbook albums have existed since the beginning of photography. However, the sad fact is that photographs and most printers material are not pernament: they will fade and yellow with age. Scrapbookers of today are aware of these problems, and the industry is providing remedies to minimize deterioration. For the best results, avoid using materials with high acid content which can cause photos and paper to deteriorate. Another thing to avoid is lignin, a metarial that is the bonding element wood fibers. Over time, paper with lignin will become yellow and brittle, so you shoud only use lignin-free products.

Your albums should contain page protectors to shield the pages from smudges, oil and dirt that can be transferred from your hand. You should never use albums with sticky "magnetic" pages. The sticky substance will be transferred to the photo and backing paper causing deterioration. Never crop Palaroid ® photos: they will curl and fall apart. Mount all memorabilia on acid-free cardstock paper, and photocopy all newspaper clipping on acid-free paper.
